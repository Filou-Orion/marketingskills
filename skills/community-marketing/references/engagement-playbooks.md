# Community Engagement Playbooks

Proven engagement tactics by community stage and platform.

---

## Engagement by Community Stage

### Stage 1: 0–50 Members (Founding)

**Goal:** Create enough activity that the community doesn't feel dead.

**Daily actions:**
- Post 1-2 conversation starters yourself
- Reply to every single post within 2 hours
- DM every new member with a personal welcome
- Tag specific members in relevant threads

**Weekly actions:**
- Host one live event (AMA, office hours, casual hangout)
- Share one piece of exclusive content
- Spotlight one member's work or win

**What to avoid:**
- Opening the community publicly too early
- Relying on members to create activity
- Having more channels/categories than active threads

---

### Stage 2: 50–500 Members (Growing)

**Goal:** Shift from founder-driven to community-driven activity.

**Daily actions:**
- Respond to threads but don't always be the first reply
- Quietly connect members with shared interests via DM
- Curate and resurface the best community content

**Weekly actions:**
- Run a recurring ritual (weekly wins thread, feedback Friday)
- Identify emerging power users and give them responsibilities
- Share community metrics internally (celebrate growth)

**Monthly actions:**
- Publish community highlights newsletter
- Run a themed event or challenge
- Review and prune inactive channels

**What to avoid:**
- Adding too many rules/structure too early
- Ignoring lurkers (they're your future contributors)
- Letting spam or low-quality posts slide

---

### Stage 3: 500–5,000 Members (Scaling)

**Goal:** Build systems and empower community leaders.

**Key shifts:**
- Hire or appoint community moderators
- Create a moderator playbook with response guidelines
- Build automated onboarding (welcome bot, intro channel workflow)
- Develop a content calendar for community posts

**Weekly actions:**
- Moderator sync meeting
- Review community health metrics
- Plan upcoming events and content themes

**Monthly actions:**
- Community survey (NPS or satisfaction)
- Recognize top contributors publicly
- Review and update community guidelines

**What to avoid:**
- Trying to do everything yourself
- Ignoring toxic behavior (it spreads fast at scale)
- Over-automating (lose the personal touch)

---

### Stage 4: 5,000+ Members (Established)

**Goal:** Sustain and deepen, create self-reinforcing loops.

**Key shifts:**
- Dedicated community team
- Subgroups or chapters by interest/region
- Community-created content programs
- Ambassador or champion program
- Community-driven support (members helping members)

## Recurring Rituals Playbook

Rituals create habit and predictability. Pick 2-3 maximum to start.

| Ritual | Cadence | Format | Best For |
|--------|---------|--------|----------|
| **Weekly wins** | Weekly | Thread: "Share your biggest win this week" | Motivation, social proof |
| **Feedback Friday** | Weekly | Thread: "Share what you're working on for feedback" | Engagement, value exchange |
| **AMA** | Biweekly | Live Q&A with expert or founder | Authority, exclusive access |
| **Monthly challenge** | Monthly | Time-boxed challenge with shared results | Activation, IKEA effect |
| **Member spotlight** | Biweekly | Featured interview or profile | Recognition, aspiration |
| **What are you reading?** | Monthly | Thread: Share books, articles, resources | Low-effort, high-value |
| **Hot takes** | Weekly | Controversial opinion + discussion | Engagement, identity |
| **Office hours** | Weekly | Live voice/video drop-in | Support, relationship |
| **Roundup newsletter** | Weekly/Monthly | Curated best-of from community | Value for lurkers |
| **Retrospective** | Quarterly | "What worked, what didn't, what's next" | Transparency, trust |

## Conversation Starters by Type

### Opinion-based (high engagement)
- "Unpopular opinion: [controversial take about your industry]. Agree or disagree?"
- "What's one tool/practice you swear by that most people overlook?"
- "If you could only keep 3 tools in your stack, which 3?"

### Experience-sharing (medium engagement)
- "What's the biggest mistake you made early on in [domain]?"
- "Share your setup/workflow for [common task]"
- "What's one thing you wish you knew when you started?"

### Help-seeking (high engagement, builds culture)
- "Has anyone solved [specific problem]? Here's what I've tried..."
- "Looking for recommendations: [specific need]"
- "Quick poll: How do you handle [common decision]?"

### Resource-sharing (builds knowledge base)
- "I just found this [resource] and it's incredible. What resources have you found recently?"
- "Building a list of [topic] resources. Drop your favorites below."

## Power User Identification

### Behavioral signals of future power users:
1. Post within first 48 hours of joining
2. Reply to other members' posts (not just their own)
3. Share original content or insights
4. Help answer questions from newer members
5. Return multiple times per week

### How to cultivate power users:
1. **Acknowledge publicly** — Thank them in front of the community
2. **Give access** — Early feature access, founder calls, private channels
3. **Give responsibility** — Moderator role, welcome committee, event host
4. **Ask for input** — "What should we do next?" gives ownership
5. **Create a inner circle** — Private group for top contributors

### Typical power user ratio:
- **1% creators**: Write original posts, create content
- **9% contributors**: Reply, react, share
- **90% lurkers**: Read only

**Goal:** Shift the ratio. Move lurkers to contributors, contributors to creators. You do this by reducing the barrier to participate and by publicly rewarding participation.
